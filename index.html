The Sunset By DaisyD, 12 Sept 2024

About Daisy

It took me a long time to reflect on this seemingly simple question: Who am I?

Am I a neuroscience student? A musician? An introvert with nothing better to do in life?

There is so much I want to share with the world and even more I want to learn from it. Yet, I’ve always been too afraid to fully reveal who I really am to anyone.

Music has always been central to my life. Growing up, it was my safe haven—a platform to store memories, to express myself, and to find a sense of belonging. I’ve made friends through songwriting, connected with new environments through making music, and documented my life through the songs I write.

But I’ve also been curious about how our brains interpret and process music. How do we recognise different genres? How do we respond to melodies, chords, lyrics, and instruments? This curiosity led me to pursue a path in science, from medical biochemistry to neuroscience.

As I’ve grown, I’ve realised how challenging it can be to chase a single dream—and how easy it is to lose your way. That’s why I’ve sought to develop various skills through activities I find therapeutic, such as painting, skating, tennis, and photography. These passions remind me of how naturally curious I am and how I’m always eager to challenge myself with things I’m not initially good at.

Here, I will be sharing my knowledge of biological sciences, art, music, and, most importantly, mental health. I hope this platform becomes a space where we can all remind ourselves that we are unique. Beyond the labels, we all have the right and the opportunity to explore who we truly are and what we are genuinely passionate about!
